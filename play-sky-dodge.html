Game Mode: Procedural High-Speed Space Arcade

Advanced Hitbox Navigation & Speed Ramping Tactics

Welcome to Sky Dodge, the high-octane endless space avoider game developed by Corelume Tech. Sky Dodge is written using vanilla JavaScript and pure canvas context operations to achieve a consistent 60fps refresh rate on desktop and mobile browsers. In this guide, we break down the underlying mathematical systems that govern procedural barrier generation, collision hitboxes, speed ramping, and tactical power-up usage.

Hitbox Calculations: Circle vs. AABB

To keep the performance lightweight and responsive, Sky Dodge does not use bloated physics libraries. Instead, it utilizes two primary collision models: **Axis-Aligned Bounding Box (AABB)** for rectangular space walls, and **Bounding Circle** intersection formulas for circular powerups and asteroid clusters. The intersection between your player orb (radius R₁ at center (x₁, y₁)) and an incoming powerup (radius R₂ at center (x₂, y₂)) is checked using Euclidean distance calculations:

Distance² = (x₂ - x₁)² + (y₂ - y₁)²
Collision = Distance² ≤ (R₁ + R₂)²

For rectangular obstacles, the engine clamps the circle's center coordinates to the closest point on the rectangle and checks if the distance is less than the circle's radius. Understanding these precise hitboxes allows you to squeeze through narrow gaps with pixel-perfect accuracy.

Near-Miss Score Multiplier

A key system that separates novice players from tournament masters is the **Near-Miss Bonus**. The engine calculates the distance to the closest active barrier every frame. If your player orb passes within **10 pixels** of a barrier without triggering a collision, the screen flashes a glowing ripple, and your score multiplier increases dramatically. This "high-risk, high-reward" mechanic incentivizes players to stay close to obstacles rather than drifting safely in empty space.

  • Speed Ramping Curve: Every 10 seconds of survival, the scroll speed of the barrier field increases by 8%. At higher speed tiers, your reaction window shrinks to under 150 milliseconds. Consistent practice is required to anticipate barriers at speed level 10+.
  • Boss Encounter Patterns: At score thresholds of 500, 1000, and 1500, the background dims, and a Boss Mothership appears. Bosses fire tracking lasers and target grids. You must dodge these tracking lines while maintaining horizontal positioning.

Tactical Powerup Configurations

Green, glowing orbs float in the obstacle lanes. Snatching them yields temporary, game-changing powerup states:

  • Energy Shield (Blue Aura): Grays out your hitbox for 5 seconds. You can pass directly through one solid red barrier without detonating, after which the shield collapses.
  • Slow-Mo (Green Pulse): Dilates time by 50% for 6 seconds. This slows down obstacle scroll speeds and tracking lasers, allowing you to easily navigate complex maze formations.
  • Cosmic Magnet (Orange Ring): Creates an electromagnetic pull around your orb, dragging in all score multiplier stars from adjacent lanes automatically for 8 seconds.
